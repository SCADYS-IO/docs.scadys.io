# PCB Design

This section covers the mechanical and electrical layout considerations of the MDD400 printed circuit board. It includes the arrangement of key components, connector placement, layer stackup, grounding strategy, thermal management, and mechanical fastening. These aspects collectively support EMC performance, mechanical integrity, manufacturability, and serviceability.

* *[Layout](layout.md)*: Describes the general arrangement of power, signal, and ground routing on the PCB. Includes placement strategies for analog/digital separation, component orientation, and high-speed signal integrity.

* *[Connectors](connectors.md)*: Lists all onboard connectors with notes on mechanical alignment, strain relief, and placement relative to housing openings and cable management.

* *[Stackup](stackup.md)*: Defines the four-layer PCB construction used in the MDD400, including signal, power, and ground planes. Stackup design supports controlled impedance routing and effective EMI containment.

* *[Earthing](earthing.md)*: Describes the grounding and shielding strategy across functional domains—logic, CAN, serial, and chassis ground. Includes net-tie details and isolation clearances.

* *[Thermal Management](thermal_management.md)*: Documents the copper pours, via stitching, and thermal relief strategies used to manage heat dissipation from regulators, power devices, and the display.

* *[Fasteners](fasteners.md)*: Specifies PCB mounting hardware, hole locations, and torque values for standoff and enclosure integration.

This section provides reference information for layout review, board fabrication, and integration into the mechanical enclosure.
