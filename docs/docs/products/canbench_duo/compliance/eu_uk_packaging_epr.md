## Scope

The European Union regulates packaging under [*Directive 94/62/EC on packaging and packaging waste*](https://environment.ec.europa.eu/topics/waste-and-recycling/packaging-and-packaging-waste_en), implemented by member states through extended producer responsibility (EPR) schemes. The United Kingdom has its own packaging waste regulations, aligned with similar EPR principles. These frameworks require producers to take responsibility for the collection, recovery, and recycling of packaging materials placed on the market.

## Why it applies

All versions of the CANBench Duo include packaging when supplied to customers, whether as a finished unit or kit. Therefore, packaging placed on the EU or UK market is subject to EPR obligations. Producers are required to register, report packaging placed on the market, and finance recovery and recycling through national compliance schemes.

## Achieving compliance

Compliance is achieved by joining a recognised packaging compliance scheme in each jurisdiction where packaging is placed on the market.

- register as a packaging producer with the relevant national authority or scheme;  
- report the weight and type of packaging materials used (paper, cardboard, plastics, metals);  
- finance the recycling and recovery of packaging materials through the scheme;  
- ensure packaging design follows the essential requirements of Directive 94/62/EC, which include minimising packaging, restricting hazardous substances, and designing for reuse and recycling.  

## Record keeping

The manufacturer must:

- keep detailed records of packaging placed on the market, broken down by material type and weight;  
- retain evidence of compliance through membership of an approved scheme;  
- provide records to national authorities or scheme operators upon request.  

## Labelling and instructions

Some EU member states and the UK require packaging to carry recycling symbols or material identification codes. The crossed-out wheeled bin symbol does not apply to packaging but to electronic equipment (WEEE). Packaging labels should follow national requirements, for example:

- material identification codes (plastic resin codes, paper recycling logos);  
- national recycling symbols (such as the “Green Dot” in some EU states, if participation in that scheme is chosen);  
- clear disposal and recycling instructions for consumers.
