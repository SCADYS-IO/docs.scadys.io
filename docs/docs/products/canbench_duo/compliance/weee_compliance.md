## Scope

The European Union regulates end-of-life management of electrical and electronic equipment under [*Directive 2012/19/EU on waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE)*](https://environment.ec.europa.eu/topics/waste-and-recycling/waste-electrical-and-electronic-equipment-weee_en). The directive places obligations on producers who place finished equipment on the EU market. The United Kingdom has equivalent legislation following EU exit.

## Why it applies

When the CANBench Duo is supplied as a finished product (Lab Grade or Pre-compliance Grade), it qualifies as electrical and electronic equipment under WEEE. This means the manufacturer is responsible for registering as a producer, marking the product, and financing collection, recycling, and environmentally sound disposal of units placed on the market. Kits and PCB assemblies are not finished apparatus and therefore do not fall under WEEE at the point of sale.

## Achieving compliance

Compliance requires fulfilling producer responsibilities in each EU member state where the product is sold:

- register as a WEEE producer with the relevant national authority or scheme;  
- mark the product with the crossed-out wheeled bin symbol;  
- provide information on safe disassembly and treatment of the product to recyclers;  
- finance the collection, recycling, and recovery of WEEE through membership of a compliance scheme.  

The United Kingdom has a similar framework under the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Regulations 2013.

## Record keeping

The manufacturer must:

- maintain registration and reporting with national WEEE schemes;  
- keep records of the number of units placed on the market and the associated financing contributions;  
- retain documentation for at least 10 years and provide to authorities upon request.

## Labelling and instructions

Finished units must bear the crossed-out wheeled bin symbol, indicating separate collection of electrical and electronic waste. If the product contains hazardous substances requiring selective treatment, additional information must be provided to treatment facilities. User instructions should advise end users not to dispose of the product with household waste and explain available collection or return options.
