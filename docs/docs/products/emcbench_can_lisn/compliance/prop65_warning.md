## Scope

The State of California regulates chemical exposure warnings under [*Proposition 65 (Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act of 1986)*](https://oehha.ca.gov/proposition-65). Proposition 65 requires businesses to provide warnings if their products expose consumers to listed chemicals above specified safe-harbor levels. The list includes more than 900 chemicals, covering substances such as lead, phthalates, and certain flame retardants.

## Why it applies

All versions of the EMCBench CAN-LISN may contain components, solder, plastics, or finishes that could include trace amounts of Proposition 65-listed substances. Even when the product complies with EU RoHS and REACH restrictions, Proposition 65 obligations can still apply because its scope is broader and includes chemicals at very low thresholds. The duty is on businesses selling into California to provide clear and reasonable warnings if exposures exceed safe-harbor levels.

## Achieving compliance

Compliance can be achieved by one of two approaches:

- conduct an exposure assessment to determine whether the product results in consumer exposures above safe-harbor levels; or  
- apply a Proposition 65 warning label if exposure assessment is not performed or if exposure cannot be ruled out.  

The commonly used short-form warning is:

>⚠️ WARNING: This product can expose you to chemicals including [name of one or more chemicals], which is known to the State of California to cause cancer and birth defects or other reproductive harm. For more information, go to www.P65Warnings.ca.gov
.

If specific chemical names are known, they should be listed in place of the bracketed text. The warning may be applied on the product, packaging, or accompanying documentation.

## Record keeping

The manufacturer should:

- maintain supplier declarations and material safety information to identify Proposition 65 substances;  
- document exposure assessments or rationale for using a warning label;  
- keep records of warnings applied and ensure they are updated if the list of chemicals changes.

## Labelling and instructions

Products sold into California must carry the required Proposition 65 warning if exposures exceed thresholds. The warning must be conspicuous, in English, and provided both online (at the point of sale) and physically with the product (label or documentation).
