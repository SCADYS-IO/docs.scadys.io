## Scope

The European Union regulates the use of chemicals in articles under [*Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006 on the Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals (REACH)*](https://echa.europa.eu/regulations/reach/authorisation/reach-authorisation-process). The relevant duty for equipment such as the EMCBench CAN-LISN is Article 33, which requires communication of information about substances of very high concern (SVHCs) when present above 0.1 % weight by weight in any article.

## Why it applies

All forms of the EMCBench CAN-LISN, including finished units, kits, and PCB assemblies, are considered articles under REACH. Therefore, the duty to communicate SVHC information applies regardless of the sales format. The requirement ensures that downstream users and consumers are informed about the presence of hazardous substances in components, connectors, PCBs, or enclosures.

## Achieving compliance

Compliance is achieved through supply chain management and disclosure:

- collect and maintain supplier declarations on REACH SVHC content for all components and materials;  
- determine whether any SVHCs above 0.1 % w/w are present in the product as supplied;  
- if present, provide the identity of the substance to customers and downstream users;  
- notify the European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) via the SCIP database if SVHCs above the threshold are present.  

Suppliers must update information when new substances are added to the Candidate List.

## Record keeping

The manufacturer must:

- maintain an up-to-date list of materials and supplier declarations;  
- keep records of SVHC assessments and communications;  
- ensure records are updated following each revision of the ECHA Candidate List (published twice per year).  

## Labelling and instructions

There is no specific REACH label. Where SVHCs are present above the threshold, the information must be made available to recipients of the article. This can be done via safety information in product documentation or via declarations published on the manufacturer’s website. For consumer requests, the required information must be provided within 45 days free of charge.
