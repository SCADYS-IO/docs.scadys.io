# K-RoHS (South Korea Hazardous Substances)

## Scope

South Korea regulates hazardous substances in electrical and electronic equipment under the [*Act for Resource Recycling of Electrical and Electronic Equipment and Vehicles*](https://www.kats.go.kr/en/kats/statutes/view.do?seq=53), administered by the Korean Agency for Technology and Standards (KATS). This is commonly referred to as “K-RoHS.” The scope is aligned with EU RoHS, covering most electrical and electronic products placed on the Korean market.

## Why it applies

SCADYS.IO retail products, when supplied as a finished unit in Korea, falls under the scope of K-RoHS. The regulation limits the same set of hazardous substances as EU RoHS, at the same thresholds. Kits and PCB assemblies are not considered finished products at the point of sale, so obligations transfer to the assembler.

## Achieving compliance

Compliance requires similar actions to EU RoHS, with additional extended producer responsibility (EPR) obligations:

- obtain RoHS compliance declarations from suppliers and confirm substances are below threshold values;  
- maintain technical documentation similar to EN IEC 63000;  
- register with the Korea Eco-Products Institute (KEPI) if required for EPR reporting;  
- participate in recycling and recovery obligations for electronic equipment placed on the market.  

## Record keeping

Manufacturers must:

- retain supplier declarations and technical files demonstrating compliance;  
- provide conformity evidence to KATS or KEPI upon request;  
- maintain records of units placed on the market to support EPR obligations.

## Labelling and instructions

Products subject to K-RoHS must bear a recycling symbol and substance disclosure markings as required by Korean regulations. This typically includes an indication of restricted substance presence and compliance status. Detailed marking guidance is published by KATS.
