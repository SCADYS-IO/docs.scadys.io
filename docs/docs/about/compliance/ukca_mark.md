# UKCA Mark (UK EMC Regulations)

## Scope

The United Kingdom regulates electromagnetic compatibility under the [*Electromagnetic Compatibility Regulations 2016*](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/emc-regulations-2016), as amended for UK law following EU exit. A finished unit placed on the UK market must meet essential EMC requirements and bear the UKCA marking. For laboratory and measurement equipment, the usual route is application of the harmonised standard [*EN/IEC 61326-1*](https://webstore.iec.ch/publication/4232).

## Why it applies

SCADYS.IO retail products, when supplied as a finished unit, is classed as apparatus under the UK EMC Regulations. Even though no digital circuits above 9 kHz are present, the assembly can generate or be affected by electromagnetic disturbance. The EMC framework ensures emissions are limited and immunity is sufficient for use in laboratory environments.

## Achieving compliance

Compliance is demonstrated by applying harmonised standards and preparing technical documentation.

- The UK framework allows self-certification: the manufacturer issues a UK Declaration of Conformity based on their own assessment.  
- Testing may be carried out in-house or at any competent facility.  
- Accredited or UKAS-certified laboratories are not required, but commonly used to strengthen evidence.  
- A conformity assessment body is only necessary if harmonised standards are not fully applied.

If an external AC/DC power supply is supplied with the product, it must be UKCA marked (e.g. assessed to EN 62368-1) and tested as part of the configuration.

## Record keeping

The manufacturer (or UK authorised representative) must:

- keep the technical documentation and UK Declaration of Conformity for at least 10 years after placing the product on the market;  
- ensure continued conformity during production;  
- supply the technical file to the UK market surveillance authority upon request.

## Labelling and instructions

Finished units must:

- bear the UKCA marking on the product or, if not possible, on packaging and accompanying documents;  
- include the manufacturer’s name, registered trade name or trademark, and postal address;  
- show a product identifier traceable to the technical file;  
- include user instructions covering intended use and any EMC-related considerations for installation and cabling.
