# RoHS (Restriction of Hazardous Substances)

## Scope

The European Union regulates restrictions on certain hazardous substances under [*Directive 2011/65/EU on the restriction of hazardous substances (RoHS)*](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2011/65/oj), as amended. The United Kingdom has equivalent regulations following EU exit. RoHS limits the concentration of specified substances in electrical and electronic equipment placed on the market, including lead, mercury, cadmium, hexavalent chromium, and certain flame retardants.

## Why it applies

When SCADYS.IO retail products is supplied as a finished unit, it is classed as electrical and electronic equipment and must comply with RoHS substance restrictions. This ensures that hazardous materials are minimised in solder, connectors, cables, and other components. Kits and PCB assemblies are not finished apparatus at the point of sale, so the compliance obligation transfers to the assembler when the unit is completed.

## Achieving compliance

Compliance with RoHS is achieved through material declarations and supplier management:

- collect and maintain RoHS compliance declarations or certificates from all component and material suppliers;  
- ensure that restricted substances are below the permitted concentration values (typically 0.1 % by weight, 0.01 % for cadmium);  
- apply conformity assessment using EN IEC 63000, which provides the documentation requirements for technical files;  
- include RoHS in procurement and design control procedures to prevent non-compliant parts from entering the supply chain.  

## Record keeping

The manufacturer must:

- maintain a technical file including supplier declarations, test reports, and conformity assessment;  
- prepare and retain an EU or UK Declaration of Conformity referencing RoHS;  
- keep records for at least 10 years after placing the product on the market.

## Labelling and instructions

There is no specific RoHS symbol. Compliance is declared through the CE or UKCA mark and the associated Declaration of Conformity. The manufacturer’s name, product identifier, and conformity documentation must accompany the finished unit. Some markets may require additional environmental labels if substances of concern are present (for example, China RoHS marking).
