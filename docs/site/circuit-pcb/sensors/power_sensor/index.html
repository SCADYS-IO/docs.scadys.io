    Power Sensor

    The MDD400 continuously monitors its own supply voltage and current draw to provide diagnostic data, support fault detection, and enable intelligent power management.

    The power sensor circuit (schematic below) uses a INA219 Current/Power Monitor With I2C Interface, located in the CAN domain. To maintain galvanic isolation from the digital domain, an ISO1541 I2C isolator transfers SDA and SCL signals across the galvanic barrier.

    The INA219's A1 and A0 address pins are both tied to ground, setting the address of the sensor on the I2C bus to 0X40.

    Voltage Sensing

    The input voltage is sensed downstream of the over-voltage protection MOSFET, which disconnects the load when the supply exceeds 18.5 V. This ensures that the INA219 is never exposed to transients or sustained voltages above its absolute maximum input rating of 26 V. The INA219's VS pin is supplied from the 5.54 V isolated rail (VPP), and its voltage measurement is taken between IN+ and IN− referenced to GNDC.

    As a result of this topology, the INA219 only measures the regulated supply voltage seen by the internal CAN domain circuitry, rather than the raw external supply. This is sufficient for diagnostic and power management purposes.

    Current Sensing

    The input current is measured using a high-side 330 mΩ shunt resistor (Yageo PT0603FR-070R33L) placed between the protected supply (VS+) and the internal supply rail (VS−). This location ensures that current is measured after the MOSFET limiter, protecting the INA219 from over-current events during external transients such as load dump (ISO 7637-2 Pulse 5b).

    At the maximum expected load current of 150 mA, the voltage drop across the shunt is 49.5 mV. The INA219 is configured for a programmable gain amplifier (PGA) setting of /4, yielding a ±80 mV full-scale input range. This provides sufficient headroom while maximizing resolution.

    The effective current measurement resolution at 12-bit is approximately 59 μA/LSB:

    • shunt voltage resolution = 80 mV / 2^12 = 19.5 μV;
    • current resolution = 19.5 μV / 0.33 Ω ≈ 59 μA/LSB.

    Peak measurable current without clipping is:

    • I_max = 80 mV / 0.33 Ω ≈ 242 mA.

    This comfortably exceeds the 150 mA peak expected load.

    Galvanic Isolation

    The INA219 is located in the CAN domain and shares a ground reference (GNDC) with other CAN-side components. The I2C signals are passed through an ISO1541 digital isolator, with VCC1/GND1 referenced to the digital logic domain (VCC/GNDREF) and VCC2/GND2 referenced to the CAN domain (VPP/GNDC).

    The I2C isolator supports bidirectional data communication (SDA), with SDA and SCL pull-up resistors (R32 and R33, both 4.7 kΩ) on the isolated side to VPP. The digital logic domain already includes pull-up resistors to VCC, as noted in the MCU and Storage section.

    Protection Considerations

    By placing the shunt resistor downstream of the 18.5 V over-voltage MOSFET limiter, the INA219 is shielded from high-voltage transients and does not require additional series resistors or transient protection devices at IN+ or IN−. This simplifies the circuit and improves measurement accuracy.
