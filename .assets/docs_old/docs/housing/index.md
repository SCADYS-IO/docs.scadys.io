# Housing

The housing section describes the mechanical enclosure and environmental sealing strategy of the MDD400. It covers the front bezel, rear case, panel interface, and sealing components that enable safe installation in marine environments. Design considerations include ingress protection, thermal expansion, mechanical robustness, and serviceability.

* *[Bezel](bezel.md)*: Describes the front housing element that frames the display and touch interface. Includes mounting flange dimensions, aesthetic treatments, and sealing surface design.

* *[Back](back.md)*: Covers the rear housing structure, including cable entry points, internal supports, and mechanical integration with the PCB and standoffs.

* *[Panel Overlay](panel_overlay.md)*: Describes the printed overlay or bonded glass panel used for labeling, visual alignment, and UV protection. This surface interfaces directly with the capacitive touch layer.

* *[Gaskets](gaskets.md)*: Documents the sealing materials used to achieve an IP-rated enclosure, including between bezel and back, and around the display cutout.

* *[Fasteners](fasteners.md)*: Lists the mechanical fasteners used in final assembly, including torque specifications and thread types.

* *[Assembly](assembly.md)*: Provides the recommended assembly sequence, tooling, and inspection checks for production.

* *[Appendices](appendices.md)* and *[Change Log](changelog.md)*: Supplementary information and revision history for housing-related changes.

This section defines the physical form factor and environmental interface of the product, ensuring compatibility with marine installation standards and long-term durability.
